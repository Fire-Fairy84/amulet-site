Bastos

Page of Wands

The new enthusiasm, arriving loud and unaccredited — the idea that won't shut up, the impulse to begin, news of fire coming. Not yet a plan and not required to be. The page's job is to say it out loud.

A young figure stands in open desert, holding a wand taller than any plan they have, studying its sprouting tip like a letter that just arrived. The clothes are covered in salamanders — fire's emblem worn before fire is mastered. Nothing around for miles. Enthusiasm this early needs no audience.

the spark bearer, enthusiasm before credentials, the message of fire, beginner's audacity, curiosity with a fuse, permission to be new

Upright

Every suit sends a page first, and fire's page arrives the way fire does: suddenly, and with no paperwork. The Page of Wands is enthusiasm at the stage before it can defend itself — the idea you cannot stop mentioning, the course tab open for a week, the pull toward a thing you have no credentials for. The card's first service is taxonomy: this is a page, not a king. It is not asking you to be qualified. It is asking you to be interested, out loud, which is a different and earlier skill.

Notice where the figure stands: desert, no roads, no audience, no infrastructure. New fire always lands in exactly this terrain — before the plan, before the mentor, before the market validates anything. The barrenness is not a flaw in the sign; it is the signature. If the ground were already built on, the fire would be someone else's. The page studies the wand's green tip because that is all there is yet: a living thing with no track record. Enthusiasms at this age cannot be evaluated, only fed or starved, and evaluation applied too early is just starving with a clipboard.

The page is also, traditionally, a messenger — and this card often means literal news: the spark arriving from outside, the invitation, the call for volunteers, the offhand comment that lights something. The message of fire has a short shelf life. It does not require an immediate yes, but it does require an immediate acknowledgment — write it down, say it to one person, take the one small step that keeps it alive past the week's end. Sparks die of administration; they also die of silence. The narrow path between is the page's whole art: small, fast, sincere motion.

What the card asks of you, finally, is tolerance for your own beginner state. The salamander suit is worn before it is earned — that is how it gets earned. There will be cards later for strategy, endurance, and freight; the suit is full of them, and they are all downstream of this one moment where someone permitted themselves to be publicly, unimpressively new. Do the small audacious thing. Send the message. Ask the naive question in the room of experts. Fire's whole future depends on somebody being willing to look foolish holding a stick in the desert.

At work

The unqualified pull toward the new domain — say it out loud, take one small public step. Interest is the credential at this stage; the rest are earned downstream.

In love

The spark, early and unaccredited — the message sent first, the crush admitted, the honest opening move. Not yet a plan; not required to be one.

In you

Permission to be new. The enthusiasm you keep half-mentioning wants one real act this week — feed it small and fast, or admit you're starving it.

Note: Not a commitment and not a life plan — a page, not a king. Nobody is asking for competence yet. Only for the message, delivered.

Reversed

The spark mishandled — swallowed unspoken, or spent in a week of enthusiasm with no second week. Beginnings collected instead of made. Somewhere, an unsent message going stale.

Reversed, the Page of Wands fails in two directions, and they look like opposites while sharing one root. The spark is either suppressed — the message never delivered — or scattered: delivered constantly, to a new address every week.

Suppression is the quieter failure. The idea came, and was met immediately by the interior committee: too late to start, too old to be new, too crowded a field, who are you to. The page's enthusiasm cannot out-argue that committee — pages never can; they lack standing. What they can do is act below the committee's radar, and this is the reversed card's practical counsel: shrink the first step until it no longer triggers review. Not the novel — one page. Not the career change — one conversation. The committee convenes only for large motions; sincerity in small denominations passes freely. The message in the bag has no expiry printed on it, but sparks kept in bags do go quietly out, and the going-out is usually mistaken, later, for never having cared.

Scatter is the louder failure and photographs better: always beginning, never continuing. The new domain every month, the funded enthusiasm abandoned at the first plateau, the identity built around starting because starting supplies what continuing cannot — the pure hit of possibility before reality has voted. This is fire's specific addiction, and the card names it without contempt: novelty is genuinely the page's element; the failure is only in never letting any spark meet its second week. The test for whether an enthusiasm deserves continuation is not intensity — intensity is week-one weather — but this: does it survive being worked on badly? The real ones tolerate your incompetence at them. The decorative ones require you to stay a beginner forever, because beginner is the only role they cast.

Both failures repair the same way: one spark, chosen, carried one week past comfortable. Past the applause of announcing, past the plateau where newness wears off, into the unglamorous second stretch where pages begin, slowly, to become knights. The desert is patient. The wand is still green. The only thing that expires is the particular hour in which sending the message was easy.

At work

Serial week-one enthusiasm, or the idea swallowed before the meeting. Shrink the step below the inner committee's radar — one conversation, one page, sent.

In love

The opening move not made, staling in the bag — or charm that restarts weekly and never continues. One spark, carried past the announcement, into week two.

In you

Starting as identity, possibility as the drug. Test the enthusiasm: does it survive being done badly? The real ones tolerate your incompetence.

Note: Not proof the spark was false. Mishandled is not mistaken — the wand is still green; what expires is the easy hour for sending.
